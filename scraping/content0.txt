            <h2>Guidelines to Write an Effective CV:</h2>
            <ol>
                <li>A candidate should include his official name, email, and address.</li>
                <li>A candidate should never use a personal pronoun i.e. ‘I’</li>
                <li>A candidate should never mention any irrelevant information like gender until and unless the
                    application requires.</li>
                <li>A candidate should never add his or her picture that may cause any biasness until and unless the
                    application requires.</li>
                <li> A candidate should mention his or her practical achievements and skills. He or she shouldn’t
                    include any experience if he doesn’t have an experience letter or certificate.</li>
                <li>A candidate should avoid complex vocabulary or flowery words. He should include simple language as
                    the recruiter judge all these professional skills.</li>
                <li>A candidate should always include work experience and qualification from the latest to the oldest.
                </li>
                <li>A candidate should review the CV for any punctuation or grammatical mistakes</li>
                <li>A candidate should never add any reference until required.</li>
                <li>A candidate should add his or her contact number with an international country code +92-123-1234567
                </li>
                <li>A candidate should never add hobbies or interests in the effective CV. Don’t write about your
                    hobbies and interests, they are not inviting you to friendship.</li>
            </ol>
            <ol start="12">
                <li>A candidate should always design a CV according to the specific job, education, or internship
                    requirements.</li>
                <li>A CV should be based on a single page but it can be 2 pages as well but should not be more than that
                    as it takes too much time to review the application that recruiters don’t have.</li>
                <li>A candidate should design the relevant CV according to the employer’s requirements.</li>
                <li>A candidate should always use PDF format for emailing CVs and Cover Letters so that page settings
                    wouldn’t be disturbed while printing.</li>
                <li>A candidate should review the CVs before applying for a specific job or place and look for the
                    keywords according to that specific job requirement. For example, a clinical Dietician professional
                    may have used words in job skills or traineeship such as, ‘counselor’, ‘assessment’, or
                    anthropometric measurements.</li>
            </ol>
